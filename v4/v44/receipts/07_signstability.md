# Receipt 07 — mean-field ROI-curl sign-stability (HQ, 2026-07-07)

Fable's red-team flagged the load-bearing failure mode: the aniso signal is *generated by*
the grid (curl of the estimated S(τy)−S(τx) field), and the harness's own config notes
"M=400 grids are NOT sign-stable." If the per-block grid curl flips sign, the 8-block mean
could self-cancel — a false null. HQ ran a quick grid-only check (no dynamics): signed
ROI-integrated mixed partial ∂²(S(τy)−S(τx))/∂x∂y per scout geometry, per fresh seed.

## Result (equal-arm-free; each seed = one block's fresh grid)
```
M_grid=400, 16 seeds:  cell A(0.1,2.0) mean -2.61 std 4.95 sign +5/-11 SNR 0.53
                       cell C(0.1,1.0) mean -2.18 std 4.86 sign +6/-10 SNR 0.45
M_grid=4000, 8 seeds:  cell A(0.1,2.0) mean +0.45 std 1.47 sign +4/-4  SNR 0.30
                       cell C(0.1,1.0) mean +0.23 std 1.11 sign +6/-2  SNR 0.21
```
(A≡B and C≡D to 4 s.f.: the ROI mixed-partial of the *small-τx* grid is ~0, so the curl is
carried by the larger-horizon τy grid — physically sensible, not a bug. tx=0.1 vs 0.25 do
not separate here.)

## Reading — INCONCLUSIVE as a clean number, but informative
- **The per-seed SNR metric is not the right instrument.** M=4000 being *less* sign-stable
  than M=400 (0.30 vs 0.53) is backwards for pure MC noise. What actually happens: the
  mixed partial amplifies grid MC noise, so at M=400 the curl magnitude is ~6× inflated
  (−2.6) and noise-dominated; at M=4000 it collapses toward the small true value (+0.45) but
  the per-seed sample still splits ~evenly. So **a single grid's ROI curl is noise-dominated
  at BOTH M** — you cannot read a reliable sign from one grid realization at either.
- **This is consistent with, not contradictory to, the harness note.** The registered v4.3
  directional prediction was seed-UNANIMOUS only because it used the *seed-averaged* mean
  field (many grids averaged → the small true curl emerges). Per-single-grid, the sign is
  unreliable — which is what "M=400 not sign-stable" means.
- **Implication for the scout:** each block uses one fresh grid, so each block's realized
  current inherits a noise-dominated grid-curl sign → low per-block SNR → the 8-block mean is
  low-SNR (not exactly self-cancelling, but weak). Raising M *per block* (400→2000) does NOT
  cleanly fix this (M=4000 per-seed is still noise-dominated). The real levers are **more
  blocks** (the decision-rule's AMBER-extension) and/or **seed-averaged grids per block** (a
  design change), not M-per-block.

## Precondition it does NOT retire (must be done properly before the run)
The correct sign precondition is the v4.3 recipe: the **seed-averaged** mean-field ROI curl
sign per candidate cell (many grids, or high M with averaging), establishing a per-cell
predicted sign for one-sided banding. This quick per-seed check does not substitute for it.
Until that is run, no scout cell has a pre-registered predicted sign (see decision-rule §3),
and the all-RED pivot must not be licensed from an M=400 null without it.
