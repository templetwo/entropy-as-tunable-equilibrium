# Prior Art and Current Research: Entropy as Engine

## Executive Summary — “Are We Reinventing the Wheel?”

**Verdict: Partially, but the project sits on defensible novel ground if reframed around its strongest contribution — the τ “horizon knob” as an experimental probe and the planned first-passage discriminator.** The underlying mechanism (the causal entropic force of Wissner-Gross & Freer 2013) and its key analytic consequence (a Boltzmann-like driven steady state p ∝ exp(βT_c S_c)) are established. The most important honest finding for the project: the “steady-state-can’t-see-kinetics” diagnosis is a **rediscovery of one of the most fundamental separations in statistical mechanics** — that equilibrium distributions under detailed balance are fixed by free energies alone and are blind to barriers, rates, and path lengths, which live only in kinetic observables (rates, mean first-passage times). This is not novel physics; it is a textbook truth (Hänggi-Talkner-Borkovec 1990; Gardiner; van Kampen). However, the project’s *application* of that truth — using it as a designed discriminator to separate “engine entropy” from “readout entropy,” and its specific failed prediction τ* ∝ L²/D — is a legitimate and instructive experimental contribution.

Three headline conclusions:

- **The “horizon knob produces migration” result is a genuine demonstration but not a conceptually new property.** That a causal-entropic agent prefers “option-rich” regions (wide, shallow wells; central positions; states with many accessible futures) is the defining, documented behavior of causal entropic forcing and its cousins (empowerment, maximum-occupancy). The explicit, controlled use of τ as a tunable experimental dial whose variation *alone* drives a measurable redistribution of steady-state mass is a clean and, as far as the surveyed literature shows, under-exploited demonstration.
- **The discriminator failure is over-determined by known theory.** Equilibrium occupancy under detailed balance depends only on the per-site S_c values (acting as an effective potential), not on the kinetics of moving between sites. The fix — switching to a dynamical/first-passage observable — is exactly the standard route the field uses to recover kinetic information.
- **The real open space is the engine-vs-readout distinction itself**, which connects directly to the modern stochastic-thermodynamics program on detecting non-equilibrium (broken detailed balance, probability currents, entropy-production rate). The project’s framing has not been crisply posed in those terms in the literature, and that is where a contribution can be made.

## A) Causal Entropic Forces — State of the Field Since 2013

**The original.** A. D. Wissner-Gross & C. E. Freer, “Causal Entropic Forces,” *Phys. Rev. Lett.* **110**, 168702 (2013), DOI 10.1103/PhysRevLett.110.168702. The paper defines a causal path entropy S_c(X, τ) over the diversity of future paths within a horizon τ, and a force F = T_c ∇_X S_c(X, τ) that drives a system toward states maximizing future path diversity. They demonstrated emergent “intelligent” behaviors (particle centering in a box, cart-pole swing-up, tool-use and social-cooperation toy puzzles). The paper is highly cited — 223 citations per Semantic Scholar (22 classed “Highly Influential”), and Grokipedia’s profile of Wissner-Gross states the 2013 PRL “has garnered over 400 citations” — and widely discussed.

**Critiques and comments (important for honesty).** The construct attracted formal criticism quickly:

- **H. J. Kappen, “Comment: Causal entropic forces,” arXiv:1312.4185 (2013).** Argues the causal entropy is state-independent and the entropic force vanishes for state-independent noise in a discrete-time formulation, and that the continuous-time description is incomplete. This is a substantive technical objection directly relevant to the project’s overdamped Langevin instantiation.
- **E. Canessa, “Comment on Phys. Rev. Lett. 110, 168702 (2013),” arXiv:1308.4375 (2013).** Argues the main relations were anticipated by earlier force–entropy correlations within a simpler statistical-thermodynamic model, and that the “maximize future path diversity” claim is only partially true; an alternative canonical-Gibbs reading exists.
- **S. Shah, “Quantum Mechanical Foundations of Causal Entropic Forces,” AGI 2014, Springer LNCS 8598.** Notes two open problems: the definition makes no contact with knowledge/rationality, and the origin of such path-based forces in classical thermodynamic ensembles is unexplained. Crucially, per Semantic Scholar, Shah shows that “maximizing future path diversity is closely related to maximizing a particular utility function over a sequence of interactions till the system attains thermodynamic equilibrium” — i.e., the reward-shaping objection the project is grappling with has been raised in the literature.

**The reward / utility equivalence question (directly relevant to the project’s discriminator).** The literature already largely concedes that causal entropic forcing is a form of intrinsic-reward maximization:

- The Shah analysis (above) explicitly equates future-path-diversity maximization with utility maximization over a sequence of interactions until equilibrium.
- In RL terms, this is the family of **state/path-entropy maximization** objectives: Hazan et al. (2019, “Provably Efficient Maximum Entropy Exploration,” ICML) introduced maximizing the entropy of the state-visitation distribution as an intrinsic objective; Berseth et al. (“SMiRL”), Liu & Abbeel (“APT”), Seo et al. (“RE3,” random-encoder state-entropy), and Mutti et al. continued it.
- Most directly: **J. Ramírez-Ruiz, D. Grytskyy, C. Mastrogiuseppe, Y. Habib, R. Moreno-Bote, “Complex behavior from intrinsic motivation to occupy future action-state path space,” *Nature Communications* 15, 6368 (2024)**, DOI 10.1038/s41467-024-49711-1 (preprint arXiv:2205.10316; ~20k accesses). This “Maximum Occupancy Principle” (MOP) states: “we abandon the idea of reward maximization and propose that the goal of behavior is maximizing occupancy of future paths of actions and states… rewards are the means to occupy path space, not the goal per se.” It is essentially a discounted, additive RL formalization of the same future-path-diversity idea behind causal entropic forces. They prove action-state path entropy is the unique occupancy measure consistent with additivity, positivity, and smoothness, give a Bellman equation and value iteration, and *explicitly cite Wissner-Gross & Freer as a causal-entropy precursor*. This is the closest published cousin to the project and should be treated as primary prior art. **The project’s “is this just reward shaping?” objection is, in fact, the consensus view: causal entropic forcing is mathematically an entropy-of-futures intrinsic reward.**

**Empowerment (closely related intrinsic motivation).** A. S. Klyubin, D. Polani, C. L. Nehaniv, “Empowerment: A Universal Agent-Centric Measure of Control” (IEEE CEC 2005) and “All Else Being Equal Be Empowered” (ECAL 2005); review: C. Salge, C. Glackin, D. Polani, “Empowerment — An Introduction,” in *Guided Self-Organization: Inception* (Springer 2014). Empowerment maximizes the channel capacity (max mutual information) between an agent’s action sequence and its future sensor states — “keep your options open.” It is the information-theoretic sibling of causal entropic forcing. Notably, Ramírez-Ruiz et al. (2024) document that empowerment agents and causal-entropy agents share a known pathology: they cling to high-control/high-diversity fixed points (e.g., the unstable upright pendulum) rather than producing diverse repertoires; and empowerment is *not* additive over paths, unlike path entropy.

**Free Energy of expected future / active inference connection.** The “expected free energy” of active inference (Friston) decomposes into pragmatic (goal/utility) and epistemic (information-gain) terms; in the absence of a utility term, minimizing expected free energy reduces to maximizing mutual information between states and observations — i.e., it coincides with empowerment-like objectives (see “Intrinsic motivation as constrained entropy maximization,” *Entropy* 27(4):372, 2025, DOI 10.3390/e27040372, which unifies active inference, empowerment, and maximum-occupancy as variants of constrained max-entropy inference). This is the dual “entropy-bounding” engine relevant to research question B(iv).

**Applications.** Beyond robotics demonstrations (cart-pole, double-pendulum stabilization via empowerment, Jung et al.), causal entropic forcing has been invoked metaphorically in swarm intelligence, collective behavior, and econophysics (e.g., the MDPI *Entropy* “Entropic Forces in Complex Systems” special issues). Empowerment and state-entropy maximization are now mainstream in deep RL exploration. We did not find a rigorous, peer-reviewed “debunking” that retracts the core mechanism; rather, the consensus is that it is a legitimate but reinterpretable intrinsic-motivation objective with known pathologies.

**Implication for the project:** The “engine entropy” mechanism is real and established; the novel contribution is *not* the mechanism but the experimental epistemology around it (the τ probe and the discriminator).

## B) The Broader Landscape — Entropy as a Driver of Organization

The project sits inside a large, mature body of non-equilibrium statistical physics. The major established frameworks:

**(i) Prigogine’s dissipative structures.** I. Prigogine and the Brussels school (Glansdorff & Prigogine 1971; Nicolis & Prigogine, *Self-Organization in Nonequilibrium Systems*, Wiley 1977) established that systems driven far from equilibrium by sustained energy/matter flux can spontaneously form ordered “dissipative structures” (Bénard convection, Belousov-Zhabotinsky oscillations); Nobel Prize 1977. Near equilibrium, Prigogine’s minimum-entropy-production theorem holds; far from equilibrium, “order through fluctuations.”  **Caveat for honesty:** the dissipative-structures program has been criticized as having limited predictive rigor; some modern authors (e.g., arXiv:2311.13749 “On Principles of Emergent Organization”) note that many of its claims have known counterexamples and that the nonlinear-dynamics/bifurcation approach has largely superseded the purely thermodynamic framing. 

**(ii) Maximum Entropy Production Principle (MEPP).** Proponents: Paltridge (climate), Dewar (information-theoretic derivations, *J. Phys. A* 2003, 2005), Martyushev & Seleznev (review, *Physics Reports* 426:1-45, 2006). Status review: **L. M. Martyushev, “Maximum entropy production principle: history and current status,” *Physics-Uspekhi* 64:558-583 (2021), DOI 10.3367/UFNe.2020.08.038819**, which reviews MEPP’s “justification and limitations” and notes it “was repeatedly and independently proposed in the mid-20th century… and proved to be extremely effective in various nonequilibrium problems.” **The honest status:** MEPP is treated in the literature as a heuristic/variational selection principle, not a proven law (Martyushev’s review notes a principle of this kind cannot be rigorously derived, and that Dewar’s information-theoretic derivation is non-rigorous and erroneous at several points). It requires local-equilibrium and bilinear flux-force assumptions. Philosophical critiques (Synthese 2023, DOI 10.1007/s11229-023-04129-y) debate its epistemic status. MEPP is about *rate of entropy production*, which is a kinetic/dissipative quantity — closer to what the project’s first-passage observable would tap than to its steady-state observable.

**(iii) Stochastic thermodynamics and modern NESS theory.** This is the most directly relevant modern framework. Key pillars:

- C. Jarzynski, “Nonequilibrium equality for free energy differences,” *PRL* 78:2690 (1997); G. E. Crooks, *PRE* 60:2721 (1999) — fluctuation theorems.
- U. Seifert, “Entropy production along a stochastic trajectory and an integral fluctuation theorem,” *PRL* 95:040602 (2005), and the major review “Stochastic thermodynamics, fluctuation theorems and molecular machines,” *Rep. Prog. Phys.* 75:126001 (2012), DOI 10.1088/0034-4885/75/12/126001. Seifert’s framework extends work/heat/entropy production to single Langevin trajectories — exactly the project’s modeling class (overdamped Langevin in a potential) — and imposes a **local detailed balance** constraint linking rates to thermodynamics. 
- **England’s “dissipation-driven adaptation” / “dissipative adaptation”:** J. L. England, “Dissipative adaptation in driven self-assembly,” *Nature Nanotechnology* 10:919-923 (2015), DOI 10.1038/nnano.2015.250, proposing results that “imply a general thermodynamic mechanism for self-organization via dissipation of absorbed work that may be applicable in a broad class of driven many-body systems”; N. Perunov, R. A. Marsland, J. L. England, “Statistical Physics of Adaptation,” *Phys. Rev. X* 6:021036 (2016). This generalizes the Boltzmann distribution to driven systems: trajectories that absorb and dissipate work reliably become more probable, producing apparent “adaptation” without selection. This is arguably the strongest established “entropy/thermodynamics as a driver of organization” framework and a key comparison point — but note it is fundamentally about *driven, work-absorbing, detailed-balance-breaking* systems, whereas the project’s overdamped position-only causal entropic force is shown to be *conservative* and to relax to an equilibrium-like Boltzmann form. This distinction is central to the engine-vs-readout question.

**(iv) Free Energy Principle / active inference (Friston).** The FEP casts self-organizing (living) systems as minimizing variational free energy / expected surprise, bounding the entropy of their sensory states.  It is the “entropy-bounding” dual to entropy-maximizing engines. As noted in (A), expected-free-energy minimization with no utility term coincides with empowerment/mutual-information maximization,  tying Friston’s framework to the project’s intrinsic-motivation cousins. Relevant: Friston (2010, *Nat. Rev. Neurosci.*); “Intrinsic motivation as constrained entropy maximization,” *Entropy* 2025.

**(v) Active matter and motility-induced phase separation (MIPS).** M. E. Cates & J. Tailleur, “Motility-Induced Phase Separation,” *Annu. Rev. Condens. Matter Phys.* 6:219-244 (2015); J. Stenhammar, “An Introduction to Motility-Induced Phase Separation,” arXiv:2112.05024. MIPS is the canonical example of organization (dense/dilute phase coexistence) arising purely from non-equilibrium single-particle dynamics — self-propelled particles accumulate where they move slowly — **with no attractive interactions and no equilibrium counterpart**,  because microscopic time-reversal symmetry / detailed balance is broken.  This is the cleanest “organization without equilibrium” benchmark and a useful contrast: MIPS is genuinely non-equilibrium (broken detailed balance), whereas the project’s position-only causal force is conservative/equilibrium-like.

**(vi) Maximum Caliber (MaxCal).** E. T. Jaynes’ path-entropy generalization of MaxEnt. Reviews: S. Pressé, K. Ghosh, J. Lee, K. A. Dill, “Principles of maximum entropy and maximum caliber in statistical physics,” *Rev. Mod. Phys.* 85:1115-1141 (2013), DOI 10.1103/RevModPhys.85.1115; Dixit et al., “Perspective: Maximum Caliber is a general variational principle for dynamical systems,” *J. Chem. Phys.* 148:010901 (2018); Ghosh, Dixit, Agozzino, Dill, “The Maximum Caliber Variational Principle for Nonequilibria,” *Annu. Rev. Phys. Chem.* 71:213 (2020). **MaxCal is the framework the causal entropic force most resembles**: maximize a path entropy over trajectories subject to dynamical constraints. The project’s S_c is essentially a (forward, horizon-limited, natural-dynamics-sampled) path entropy. MaxCal is the proper theoretical home for the project’s quantity, and the project should position S_c explicitly as a constrained path-entropy / caliber object.

**Implication:** The project’s intellectual neighborhood is well-populated. Its quantity is a path entropy (MaxCal family); its mechanism is an intrinsic-reward force (RL/empowerment family); its aspiration (entropy as a driver) overlaps England, Prigogine, MEPP, and active matter. None of these is being reinvented wholesale, but the project must cite them to avoid appearing to.

## C) The Steady-State vs Kinetic Discriminator Problem (the core question)

**This is where the project’s “diagnosis” recapitulates a foundational, rigorously established result.**

**The thermodynamics/kinetics separation.** For an overdamped Langevin (Smoluchowski) particle, the stationary distribution is p_eq(x) ∝ exp(−βU(x)) — a functional of the *local potential value only*. It is completely insensitive to where barriers sit, how high they are, or how far apart wells are; only the well/state free energies (and their differences) matter. This is the elementary consequence of detailed balance: at equilibrium there is zero net probability current between any pair of states, so the occupancy ratio between two states depends only on their free-energy difference, not on the transition-state height or path between them. Standard references: C. W. Gardiner, *Stochastic Methods* (4th ed., Springer 2009); N. G. van Kampen, *Stochastic Processes in Physics and Chemistry* (Elsevier); R. Zwanzig, *Nonequilibrium Statistical Mechanics* (Oxford 2001).

The flip side: **kinetics carries the barrier/path information that equilibrium discards.** This is precisely the domain of Kramers reaction-rate theory. Authoritative review: **P. Hänggi, P. Talkner, M. Borkovec, “Reaction-rate theory: fifty years after Kramers,” *Rev. Mod. Phys.* 62:251-341 (1990), DOI 10.1103/RevModPhys.62.251** (building on H. A. Kramers, *Physica* 7:284-304, 1940, DOI 10.1016/S0031-8914(40)90098-2). The escape rate carries the barrier height via the Arrhenius/Kramers factor exp(−ΔU/k_BT)  times a friction- and curvature-dependent prefactor; the corresponding mean first-passage time scales as τ ∝ exp(+ΔU/k_BT). **So the project’s diagnosis — “a steady-state observable can’t see channel traversal time because equilibrium occupancy depends only on the S_c values at each location, not on the kinetic path between them” — is a direct restatement of the detailed-balance/Kramers separation. It is correct, but it is textbook, not novel.**

**MFPT theory and the L²/D scaling (the project’s failed prediction).** The expected scaling τ* ∝ L²/D for diffusive traversal of a connecting channel is the canonical diffusive first-passage scaling: for free 1D diffusion across a domain of length L the MFPT is ⟨τ⟩ = L²/2D.  Established results the project should cite:

- **Kramers escape rate / barrier-dominated MFPT** (Hänggi-Talkner-Borkovec 1990).
- **Narrow escape problem:** D. Holcman & Z. Schuss, “The narrow escape problem,” *SIAM Review* 56(2):213-257 (2014), DOI 10.1137/120898395; Singer, Schuss, Holcman (*J. Stat. Phys.* 122, 2006). MFPT to a small window  scales as ⟨τ⟩ ≈ (|Ω|/πD)ln(1/ε) in 2D and |Ω|/(4aD) in 3D — diffusive, ∝ domain size / D.
- **Geometry-controlled kinetics:** O. Bénichou & R. Voituriez, “From first-passage times of random walks in confinement to geometry-controlled kinetics,” *Physics Reports* 539(4):225-284 (2014), DOI 10.1016/j.physrep.2014.02.003; and the origin paper Condamin, Bénichou, Tejedor, Voituriez, Klafter, “First-passage times in complex scale-invariant media,” *Nature* 450:77-80 (2007), DOI 10.1038/nature06201, which establishes ⟨T⟩ ∝ V (confining volume) for diffusion.
- Berg & Purcell (1977) diffusion-to-capture is the biophysical antecedent for diffusion-limited target finding.

**Why the project’s discriminator failed — and why that is unsurprising.** The project predicted τ* ∝ L²/D (channel length controls the crossover) but found τ* flat in channel length and instead set by 1/D and large-chamber self-exploration size. This is exactly what detailed balance forces: a steady-state occupancy is an equilibrium-like distribution determined by the per-location S_c (an effective potential); the channel traversal time L²/D is a *kinetic* quantity that the equilibrium occupancy is provably blind to. The crossover the project did see (set by chamber self-exploration size, ∝ 1/D) reflects when the horizon τ is long enough for the within-chamber future-path-counting to register the geometry — a property of the local S_c landscape, not of inter-chamber transport. **The fix the project proposes — use a dynamical first-passage observable — is precisely the established method for recovering kinetic information that equilibrium discards.** Using MFPT/dynamical observables to fingerprint mechanisms (vs. equilibrium occupancy) is standard methodology in single-molecule biophysics and reaction-rate theory.

**Implication:** The project should explicitly frame the v1 discriminator failure as a confirmation of the thermodynamics/kinetics separation, cite Hänggi et al. and the MFPT canon, and present v2 as a principled application rather than a discovery of a new effect.

## D) Distinguishing Driven/Non-Equilibrium Systems from Equilibrium — The Detection Problem

This is the literature that most directly informs a *better* engine-vs-readout discriminator. The modern stochastic-thermodynamics toolkit for proving a system is genuinely driven (not merely relaxing) rests on:

**Broken detailed balance and probability currents.** At equilibrium, detailed balance holds: zero net probability current between states, no circulating loops in phase space. A non-equilibrium steady state (NESS) breaks this: steady-state probability currents/circulation appear. Canonical review: **F. S. Gnesotto, F. Mura, J. Gladrow, C. P. Broedersz, “Broken detailed balance and non-equilibrium dynamics in living systems: a review,” *Rep. Prog. Phys.* 81:066601 (2018), DOI 10.1088/1361-6633/aab3ed.** Foundational experiment: **C. Battle, C. P. Broedersz, N. Fakhri, V. F. Geyer, J. Howard, C. F. Schmidt & F. C. MacKintosh, “Broken detailed balance at mesoscopic scales in active biological systems,” *Science* 352(6285):604-607, 29 Apr 2016, DOI 10.1126/science.aac8167**; the method “unambiguously identif[ies] which random fluctuations at the cellular scale are out of equilibrium… in the absence of periodic motion” by measuring phase-space probability flux loops — even when the stationary distribution looks thermal.

**Entropy production rate as the order parameter of non-equilibrium.** The steady-state entropy production rate is strictly positive iff detailed balance is broken; it is the natural scalar “am I driven?” measure (Seifert 2012; Nardini et al., “Entropy production in field theories without time-reversal symmetry,” *PRX* 7:021007, 2017, for active matter). Estimators: J. Li, J. M. Horowitz, T. R. Gingrich, N. Fakhri, “Quantifying dissipation using fluctuating currents,” *Nat. Commun.* 10:1666 (2019). 

**Thermodynamic uncertainty relations (TUR).** A. C. Barato & U. Seifert (*PRL* 114:158101, 2015); Horowitz & Gingrich, “Thermodynamic uncertainty relations constrain non-equilibrium fluctuations,” *Nat. Phys.* 16:15-20 (2020); Pietzonka, Ritort, Seifert, “Finite-time generalization of the TUR,” *PRE* 96:012101 (2017). The TUR lower-bounds current fluctuations by the entropy production, providing a way to *infer* dissipation (hence driving) from observable current statistics  — including first-passage-time fluctuations (Gingrich & Horowitz, “Fundamental bounds on first passage time fluctuations for currents,” *PRL* 119:170601, 2017).

**Fluctuation-dissipation theorem (FDT) violation.** At equilibrium, response and correlation are linked by the FDT; its violation is a classic non-equilibrium signature (Harada-Sasa equality relating FDT violation to dissipation, *PRL* 95:130602, 2005).

**Implication for the project (important):** There is a strong case that a *better* engine-vs-readout discriminator already exists in the form of entropy-production / probability-current / FDT-violation measures. **But there is a subtlety the project must confront honestly:** its own finding is that the overdamped position-only causal entropic force is *conservative* and yields an equilibrium Boltzmann-like steady state p ∝ exp(βT_c S_c). A conservative force with a Boltzmann steady state has *zero* steady-state probability current and *zero* entropy production — it would register as “at equilibrium” under every detection method in this section. This is the deepest tension in the whole project: **in the position-only overdamped regime, “engine entropy” is mathematically indistinguishable from a “readout” relaxation to an effective potential U_eff = −T_c S_c.** The τ-dependence of U_eff is the only thing that distinguishes it from a thermal potential, which is exactly why the τ-knob (not a current/entropy-production measure) is the project’s genuinely distinguishing probe. The first-passage observable proposed for v2 will detect the *kinetic* fingerprint of the τ-shaped landscape, not broken detailed balance per se.

## E) Novelty Assessment — Honest Accounting

**What is NOT novel (re-derivation / re-instantiation of established results):**

1. **The causal entropic force mechanism itself** — Wissner-Gross & Freer 2013, with known critiques (Kappen, Canessa) and a known reframing as intrinsic reward (Shah 2014; Ramírez-Ruiz et al. 2024).
1. **The “option-rich preference” / migration toward high-future-diversity states** — this is the *defining* documented behavior of causal entropic forcing, empowerment, and maximum-occupancy. A causal-entropy agent preferring wide shallow wells over deep narrow ones is the 1D analogue of the box-centering result in the original paper.
1. **The conservative-force Boltzmann steady state p ∝ exp(βT_c S_c)** — this is the expected result for any conservative force; it is the gradient-of-a-scalar structure, and matches the canonical Smoluchowski stationary distribution.
1. **The “steady-state can’t see kinetics” diagnosis** — a rediscovery of the detailed-balance/thermodynamics-kinetics separation (Hänggi et al. 1990; Gardiner; van Kampen). Equilibrium occupancy ∝ exp(−βU_eff) is provably blind to barriers and path lengths.
1. **The L²/D channel MFPT scaling** — textbook diffusive first-passage (Redner; Bénichou-Voituriez; Holcman-Schuss).
1. **Using MFPT/dynamical observables to recover kinetic mechanism** — standard methodology in reaction-rate theory and single-molecule biophysics.

**What IS genuinely novel or contributory (defensible framing):**

1. **The τ “horizon knob” as a controlled experimental probe.** While τ-dependence is implicit in causal entropic forcing, the explicit experimental program of *holding everything fixed and varying only τ* to demonstrate that organization changes with foresight alone — and framing this as the operational signature of “engine” vs “readout” entropy (thermal relaxation has no τ) — is a clean, pedagogically powerful, and (in the surveyed literature) under-exploited demonstration. This is the project’s strongest claim to originality.
1. **The explicit “engine vs readout entropy” conceptual dichotomy** posed as an empirically testable question. The stochastic-thermodynamics community has rigorous detection tools (Section D), but the specific framing of “is entropy a prime mover or a description?” tied to the τ-knob has not, to our knowledge, been crisply posed and tested this way.
1. **The designed-and-failed discriminator as a methodological contribution.** A pre-registered prediction (τ* ∝ L²/D) that *failed* for a principled reason, with a correct diagnosis and a principled fix, is good science and a genuine (if modest) contribution to how one should test such claims.
1. **A candidate clean model system** (overdamped Langevin, two chambers + channel, horizon knob) for studying when foresight-driven forces leave detectable kinetic vs thermodynamic signatures.

**Where the actual open space is:**

- **Resolving the conservative-force tension (Section D).** The sharpest open question: in regimes where the causal entropic force becomes *non-conservative* (e.g., velocity/momentum-dependent, or with absorbing/non-reciprocal future-sampling), does it break detailed balance and produce measurable steady-state currents / entropy production? If so, the engine-vs-readout distinction becomes detectable by standard NESS measures, and the project could connect rigorously to Seifert/Broedersz tooling. If not, the distinction is genuinely only visible kinetically (via first-passage), which is itself a publishable clarifying result.
- **Whether the τ-knob produces a *kinetic* signature no static effective potential can mimic.** The v2 first-passage experiment can test whether the τ-shaped landscape leaves a first-passage fingerprint distinguishable from any fixed thermal U(x) — i.e., whether engine entropy is operationally distinct from readout entropy at the level of dynamics even when it is indistinguishable at the level of occupancy.

## Implications for v2 (First-Passage Discriminator)

The literature strongly endorses the project’s pivot to a dynamical observable, and sharpens how to do it:

1. **Adopt MFPT and its full distribution, not just the mean.** Cite the canonical MFPT framework (Redner, *A Guide to First-Passage Processes*, Cambridge 2001; Gardiner) and the channel-scaling results (Bénichou-Voituriez 2014; Holcman-Schuss 2014). The L²/D scaling that v1 wrongly attributed to steady-state occupancy *will* appear in the MFPT — that is the whole point. The v2 prediction should be that the engine’s first-passage time between chambers carries the channel-length signature that occupancy cannot.
1. **Measure first-passage-time *fluctuations*, not only means, and connect to the TUR.** Gingrich & Horowitz (*PRL* 119:170601, 2017) and the finite-time TUR (Pietzonka-Ritort-Seifert 2017) make first-passage fluctuations a thermodynamically meaningful observable. If the τ-driven dynamics ever break detailed balance, the TUR gives a route to *infer* the implied dissipation — a quantitative “engine” signature.
1. **Test for broken detailed balance directly.** Apply the Battle/Broedersz probability-current method (and entropy-production estimators, Li et al. 2019) to the 2D two-chamber system. The predicted outcome, given v1’s conservative-force finding, is *no* detectable current in the position-only overdamped case — which would be an important negative result establishing that engine entropy in this regime is equilibrium-like and only kinetically distinguishable. A *positive* current would be a major result and should prompt re-examination of whether the implemented force is truly conservative.
1. **Position S_c explicitly as a Maximum-Caliber / path-entropy object** (Pressé et al. 2013; Ghosh et al. 2020) and the driving force as an intrinsic-reward gradient in the sense of Ramírez-Ruiz et al. (2024) and the state-entropy-maximization RL literature. This preempts the “reward shaping in disguise” objection by conceding it and reframing the contribution as *the physics of what such a path-entropy reward does to a Langevin system’s dynamics*, which is genuinely under-explored.
1. **Confront the conservative-force/zero-current tension head-on** in the writeup. The honest, strong version of the project’s thesis is: *engine entropy and readout entropy can be thermodynamically indistinguishable (identical occupancy, zero current) yet kinetically distinguishable (different first-passage signatures under a τ-knob).* That is a sharp, testable, and non-trivial claim that none of the surveyed prior art states in this form.

**Benchmarks that would change the recommendations:**

- *If* v2 finds a τ-dependent first-passage signature not reproducible by any static U(x) → strong novelty; pursue publication around the engine/readout kinetic distinction.
- *If* a probability-current/entropy-production test comes back nonzero → the force is effectively non-conservative; pivot to the standard NESS-detection framing and compare directly with England/active-matter.
- *If* both come back null and the MFPT merely reproduces textbook L²/D Kramers scaling with U_eff = −T_c S_c → the contribution collapses to a (still useful) pedagogical demonstration that causal-entropic “engine” behavior in this regime is equilibrium-equivalent; reframe accordingly and avoid over-claiming.

**Bottom line:** The project is not reinventing the causal entropic force, the path-entropy reward, or the thermodynamics-kinetics separation — all are established. It *is* doing something defensible by (a) using τ as a controlled experimental dial, (b) honestly diagnosing why a steady-state discriminator failed, and (c) pivoting to first-passage observables. The highest-value, most novel target is the precise operational question of whether τ-driven “engine” organization is distinguishable from equilibrium “readout” relaxation — and at what level (occupancy, current/entropy-production, or first-passage kinetics) that distinction lives.

## Caveats

- **Citation-count figures conflict across sources:** Semantic Scholar lists 223 citations for Wissner-Gross & Freer (2013) while Grokipedia states “over 400.” The true count depends on the database and date; treat the order of magnitude (hundreds) as reliable, the exact figure as not.
- **The MEPP “cannot be proved” characterization** reflects the general thrust of Martyushev’s 2021 review (which discusses MEPP’s limitations and the non-rigor of Dewar’s derivation) rather than a verified verbatim quotation from the article body; the exact phrasing was not confirmed in the accessible text.
- **Textbook claims (Gardiner, van Kampen, Zwanzig, Redner) for the equilibrium-vs-kinetics separation and the ⟨τ⟩ = L²/2D scaling** are near-axiomatic in stochastic-process theory; no single review states “equilibrium is independent of the barrier” verbatim because it is treated as elementary. The pairing of the Smoluchowski stationary distribution (∝ e^{−βU}) with the Kramers/MFPT barrier-dependent kinetics (Hänggi-Talkner-Borkovec 1990) is the rigorous primary support.
- **Several supporting items (Hazan et al. 2019; Berseth/Liu-Abbeel/Seo state-entropy RL; Harada-Sasa; Barato-Seifert TUR; Nardini et al. 2017; Berg-Purcell 1977; Redner 2001)** were drawn from reference lists and standard knowledge rather than fetched in full during this review; their DOIs/venues should be verified before formal publication.
- **The interpretation of the project’s own v1 results** (conservative force, zero current, τ*-scaling) is taken from the project context as given; the literature analysis assumes those internal findings are correct. The strongest external check is the direct probability-current/entropy-production test recommended for v2.
- This review prioritized peer-reviewed papers, arXiv preprints, and authoritative reviews; popular-science and blog sources encountered during searching were used only for orientation and are not relied upon for any factual claim above.
