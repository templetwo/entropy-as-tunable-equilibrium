## Description (paste into the Description field)

> **Does entropy drive a system, or only describe one?** This work tests the
> causal entropic force F = T_c ∇S_c(x, τ) of Wissner-Gross and Freer (2013)
> against ordinary thermal relaxation, using a single device as discriminator:
> a *horizon knob* τ. A readout (thermometer) view of entropy has no τ; an
> engine (prime-mover) view does. If a system can be made to organize itself
> purely by changing how far ahead it looks, entropy is doing work, not merely
> being measured.
>
> Working in an overdamped Langevin system on a one-dimensional two-well
> landscape and a corrected corridor geometry, we find that the horizon knob is
> real and **kinetically visible** — engine first-passage times across a
> corridor decrease monotonically as τ grows — yet the engine is
> **thermodynamically indistinguishable from equilibrium**: it carries zero
> probability current, and at each fixed τ it is, by the conservativeness of the
> overdamped causal force, exactly a Boltzmann distribution in the effective
> potential U_eff(x, τ) = −T_c S_c(x, τ). The engine is therefore a
> **τ-tunable equilibrium**, not an irreducible non-equilibrium engine. Engine
> transport is further found to be drift-dominated (first-passage exponent
> α ≈ 1) rather than diffusive (α ≈ 2), so the scaling cannot by itself exclude
> a "disguised reward." A rotational positive control with broken detailed
> balance confirms that the observables *do* detect genuine non-equilibrium —
> nonzero current and direction-dependent first passage — when it is present.
>
> We conclude that in the conservative, overdamped, single-coordinate regime the
> entropic horizon knob leaves **no irreducible kinetic fingerprint**, and we
> identify the non-conservative, multi-dimensional regime — where the causal
> force can have nonzero curl and sustain steady currents — as the only setting
> in which a genuine entropic engine could exist. The result is a clean,
> falsifiable negative.
>
> **Method note.** The result was produced and hardened under an explicitly
> adversarial, verification-first multi-agent protocol. Production simulations
> and figures were generated by Grok Heavy and Grok Build (xAI) against the
> public repository code; experimental design, theory, and synthesis by Claude
> (Anthropic, Opus 4.8); and independent adversarial code review by Claude Code
> (Anthropic), which caught and corrected a substantive scaling-exponent error
> and removed a circular reducibility test. The central scientific correction in
> the paper was produced by cross-review, not by any single agent — convergence
> across independent adversarial reviewers is treated as signal, and any single
> agent asserting completion is not. Research direction, framing, and final
> authority: Anthony Vasquez (The Temple of Two).
>
> All code, the production data manifest, the figure-generation script, and the
> figures are openly available and regenerate from a committed manifest with
> recorded seeds and confidence intervals.
